MOVIE REVIEW

by Naomi Castro



Synopsis: Cast & Directors: Movie Reviews: Personal Rating:
In the colorful and seemingly ideal world of Barbie Land, Barbie and Ken are having the time of their lives. But as soon as they get an opportunity to visit the actual world, they learn about both the advantages and risks of living with people.

Cast:

Margot Robbie, Ryan Gosling, Issa Rae

Director:

Greta Gerwig

Duration: 1h 54mins

 This movie is one of my favorites, of all time. It shows the struggles of being a woman and, also, shows the struggles of being a man. Its message, to not follow stereotypes, impacted me quite a lot. It shows our society's issues, the discrimination, the lack of equality and many more. It doesn't matter whether you are male or female because in this movie, I believe, anyone can understand or relate to it.

4.5

A rookie bunny cop and a cunning con artist fox must collaborate to discover a plot in a city full of anthropomorphic animals.

Cast:

Ginnifer Goodwin, Jason Bateman, Idris Elba

Director:

Byron Howard, Rich Moore, Jared Blush

Duration: 1h 48mins

 This movie was one of my favorites growing up as a kid. It shows that no matter how small, how big, or whatever animal (race) you are; you can surely achieve what the other can. It shows that everyone has the right to achieve and strive for their dreams no matter who or what they are. And despite our differences we can work together as a team.

3.5

Primo and George, a young couple, are in a long-term relationship and are already making plans for their future together.

Cast:

Kathryn Bernardo, Daniel Padilla

Director:

Cathy Garcia-Sampana

Duration: 2h

 This movie will always be part of my top 3. It shows the reality in building and having a relationship. It consists of sacrifices and pain. It appeared that no matter how long you and that person will be together, the future will always be unknown. This tells us that relationships are a huge risk and takes a lot of commitment. Which is why it is in my top 3.
